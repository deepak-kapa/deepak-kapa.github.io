These are some research directions and project ideas I'm interested in exploring. Feel free to reach out if you're interested in collaborating on any of these topics!

## ü§ñ Advanced Actuator Systems

### High-Torque Density Actuators for Micro-Robotics
<div class="idea-box">
  <p><strong>Motivation:</strong> Current miniature robots lack sufficient torque for complex manipulation tasks.</p>
  <p><strong>Approach:</strong> Develop ultra-compact planetary actuators with torque densities >100 Nm/kg for robots under 1kg.</p>
  <p><strong>Challenges:</strong> Manufacturing precision at small scales, heat dissipation, cost-effective production.</p>
  <p><strong>Potential Impact:</strong> Enable new applications in medical robotics, inspection, and swarm robotics.</p>
</div>

### Variable Stiffness Actuators for Adaptive Locomotion
<div class="idea-box">
  <p><strong>Motivation:</strong> Fixed-stiffness actuators limit adaptability to different terrains.</p>
  <p><strong>Approach:</strong> Design a compact variable stiffness mechanism integrated with planetary gearboxes.</p>
  <p><strong>Challenges:</strong> Additional complexity, control algorithms, mechanical reliability.</p>
  <p><strong>Potential Impact:</strong> Improved energy efficiency and terrain adaptability in legged robots.</p>
</div>

## ü¶ø Novel Leg Mechanisms

### Multi-Modal Leg Architecture
<div class="idea-box">
  <p><strong>Motivation:</strong> Current legged robots are optimized for a single locomotion mode.</p>
  <p><strong>Approach:</strong> Design leg mechanisms that can morph between configurations (walking, running, jumping, climbing).</p>
  <p><strong>Challenges:</strong> Mechanical complexity, switching control, structural reliability.</p>
  <p><strong>Potential Impact:</strong> Versatile robots capable of diverse tasks without mode-specific hardware.</p>
</div>

### Energy-Regenerative Suspension System
<div class="idea-box">
  <p><strong>Motivation:</strong> Energy loss during landing impacts in legged robots.</p>
  <p><strong>Approach:</strong> Integrate regenerative shock absorbers with energy recovery mechanisms.</p>
  <p><strong>Challenges:</strong> Efficiency, weight addition, control integration.</p>
  <p><strong>Potential Impact:</strong> Extended operation time for battery-powered robots.</p>
</div>

## üîß Design Optimization

### AI-Driven Actuator Design Framework
<div class="idea-box">
  <p><strong>Motivation:</strong> Traditional design optimization is time-consuming and limited by human intuition.</p>
  <p><strong>Approach:</strong> Develop ML-based generative design tools specifically for actuator systems.</p>
  <p><strong>Challenges:</strong> Training data generation, physical constraint enforcement, manufacturability.</p>
  <p><strong>Potential Impact:</strong> Accelerate development of novel actuator configurations.</p>
</div>

## üåç Sustainability in Robotics

### Bio-Degradable Robotic Components
<div class="idea-box">
  <p><strong>Motivation:</strong> Environmental impact of disposable robots (e.g., search and rescue, agriculture).</p>
  <p><strong>Approach:</strong> Research bio-degradable materials suitable for structural and mechanical components.</p>
  <p><strong>Challenges:</strong> Material strength, durability during operation, controlled degradation.</p>
  <p><strong>Potential Impact:</strong> Reduce environmental footprint of robotic applications.</p>
</div>

## üéì Interdisciplinary Projects

### Robotic Prosthetics with Natural Motion
<div class="idea-box">
  <p><strong>Motivation:</strong> Current prosthetics lack the fluidity and energy efficiency of biological limbs.</p>
  <p><strong>Approach:</strong> Apply legged robotics principles to develop biomimetic prosthetic legs.</p>
  <p><strong>Challenges:</strong> User interface, real-time adaptation, weight constraints, cost.</p>
  <p><strong>Potential Impact:</strong> Improved quality of life for amputees.</p>
</div>
